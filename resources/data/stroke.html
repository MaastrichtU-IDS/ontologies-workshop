Stroke information

Description

A stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of your brain is interrupted or reduced, depriving brain tissue of oxygen and nutrients. Within minutes, brain cells begin to die. A stroke is a medical emergency. Prompt treatment is crucial. Early action can minimize brain damage and potential complications.

Symptoms

  • You may slur your words or have difficulty understanding speech
  • Trouble with speaking and understanding. You may experience confusion
  • Numbness of the face
  • Numbness of the legs
  • Numbness of the arms
  • You may suddenly have blurred or blackened vision in one or both eyes, or you may see double
  • Headache
  • Lightheadedness or sudden dizziness
  • Trouble with walking. You may stumble or experience sudden loss of balance or loss of coordination.
